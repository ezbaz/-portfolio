Luminarium Theatre

It started with a name.

A visual identity built from light, structure, and restraint.

Intro

Luminarium is a theatre and acting school based in Vancouver. The task was to create an identity that would feel contemporary, clear, and culturally self-aware — without falling into theatrical cliché.

The name

The project began with the name Luminarium.

It suggested light, space, and a place where something becomes visible. It also carried well across languages, which mattered for a multilingual theatre environment.

Avoiding the obvious

An early direction leaned toward a lighthouse. It was clear, but too literal.

Instead of illustrating the idea, I moved toward a more restrained system — one built around the word itself.

The primary mark

The main logo is typographic.

Clean, stable, and deliberately restrained, it works as the core mark across formal and everyday applications.

The emblem

The emblem introduces a more expressive layer.

A circle, a beam, and a diagonal shift turn the wordmark into something more theatrical — not through decoration, but through focus and movement.

The system

The identity was designed to hold different tones and applications while staying recognisable.

Quiet when needed.
Expressive when necessary.
Always structured.

Closing

The result is a logo system that gives Luminarium a clear foundation without reducing it to familiar symbols.
